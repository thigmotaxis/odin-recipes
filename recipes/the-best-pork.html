Pressure Cooker Garlicky Cuban Pork

Return home

Yield: 8-10 servings

Time: 2 1/2 hours plus 1 hour marinating

Description

There is some pork. You put it in a pot. Then you take it out of the pot and put it inside yourself.

Ingredients

  • One 4-5 lbs boneless pork shoulder, cut into 4 pieces
  • 12-15 garlic cloves
  • Juice of 1 grapefruit (approx 2/3 cups)
  • 3 tbs EVOO
  • 2 tbs light brown sugar
  • 1 tbs fresh oregano leaves (or 1/2 tbs dried)
  • 2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 1/2 tbs kosher salt plus more if you feel like it
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Cilantro, chopped for serving
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Hot sauce, for serving
  • Tortillas if you feel you need the crutch of a wrapper
  • Salsa (pico de gallo, verde, etc.)

Preparation

  1. In a blender or small food processor, combine the garlic, grapefruit juice, lime zest and juice, 2 tbs EVOO, brown sugar, oregano, cumin, and salt
  2. Process until blended, then transfer to a large bowl and add the pork and bay leaf
  3. Toss to combine, then allow to marinate, covered, for at least one hour
  4. Take a break! Read a book! Say "hi" to your wife! Tell your cat it is special (even though it isn't)
  5. Heat the rest of the EVOO using the pressure cooker's saute function on medium
  6. Remove the pork from the marinade, reserving the marinade. Cook the pork in batches until it is browned on all sides (approx. 12 minutes total), deglazing as needed
  7. When all pork is browned, return it to the pot along with the reserved marinade and bay leaf
  8. Cover and cook on high pressure for 80 minutes and let the pressure release naturally
  9. Take another break! Apologize to your cat for inflating its ego and consequently leaving it unprepared for the sharp edges of adulthood
  10. Remove the pork and the bay leaf, then reduce the jus by boiling it for 10-15 minutes using the saute function
  11. Shred the pork, then toss with the jus
  12. Eat it in the manner which brings you the most joy
