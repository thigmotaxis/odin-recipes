Shakshuka with Feta

Return Home

Yield: 3-4 servings

Time: 50 minutes

Description

Would you like to read an 18 paragraph story about how my grandmother used to make this dish for me and how that experience has shaped me as a person? No? Are you sure? Because it might be pretty crucial information.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbs EVOO
  • 1 large yellow onion, halved and thinly sliced
  • 1 large red bell pepper, seeded and thinly sliced
  • 6-7 ripe roma tomatoes and their juices, coarsely chopped
  • 5 oz crumbled feta cheese
  • 6 large eggs
  • 8 garlic cloves
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1/4 tsp ground cayenne
  • 3/4 tsp kosher salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • chopped cilantro, for garnish

Preparation

  1. Heat oil in a large cast iron skillet over medium low heat. Add the onion and bell pepper and cook gently approx. 20 minutes until very soft
  2. Preheat oven to 375 degrees and remove eggs from refrigerator. Place eggs in bowl of warm water
  3. Add the garlic and cook approx. 1-2 minutes until tender
  4. Stir in cumin, paprika, and cayenne and cook 1 minute
  5. Pour in tomatoes and season with 3/4 teaspoon salt and 1/4 tsp black pepper, then simmed approx. 10 minutes until tomatoes have thickened
  6. Taste and add more salt/pepper if needed. Stir in feta cheese
  7. Gently crack eggs into the skillet. Season eggs with salt/pepper if desired
  8. Transfer skillet to the oven and bake approx. 7-10 minutes until eggs are just set
  9. Sprinkle with cilantro and serve
